# Realistic Timelines

The fact base for rule R3. Flag any promise faster than these; treat anything slower as honest.

## Local SEO / Google

| Work | Honest timeline |
|---|---|
| Technical fixes (schema, titles, indexation, Google Business Profile cleanup) | Live in week 1–2; these are within the vendor's control and CAN be committed to |
| First measurable movement (impressions, map-pack appearances) | 6–12 weeks |
| Meaningful ranking gains in a competitive local market | 3–6 months |
| Compounding content results | 6–12 months |

## GEO / AI visibility (ChatGPT, Perplexity, AI Overviews)

| Work | Honest timeline |
|---|---|
| Crawler access, schema, Bing indexation fixed | Week 1; verifiable in days |
| First AI citations appearing | 6–8 weeks |
| Reliable, repeatable AI visibility | 3–6 months |

AI answers vary run to run — single-day "we got you into ChatGPT" claims are noise. Baseline measurement needs at least a week of tracking.

## Automation / AI service builds (voice agents, chatbots, workflow automation)

These are deliverables, not algorithm outcomes — the timeline logic inverts.

| Work | Honest timeline |
|---|---|
| Simple chatbot or missed-call text-back, one channel | 1–2 weeks to live |
| AI receptionist / voice agent with booking + calendar integration | 2–4 weeks to live, then a 2–4 week tuning period on real calls |
| Multi-step workflow automation (lead intake → CRM → follow-up) | 2–6 weeks depending on how many systems it touches |

What CAN be committed to: delivery dates, what the system does on day one, a named tuning period, and a response/fix window when it breaks. What still can't: "never misses a call" (systems fail), "replaces your receptionist" or a labor-savings percentage with no math, and accuracy claims stated as absolutes. AI systems mishear and misfire — an honest proposal names the human fallback (transfer to a person, daily review of transcripts), which builds more trust than pretending failure is impossible.

## What may never be promised

- Specific rankings or positions ("page one," "top 3")
- Being cited by a specific AI engine by a specific date
- Lead or revenue counts
- Anything dependent on a third party's algorithm

The only honest commitments are inputs (work delivered, on a schedule) and process (measurement, reporting, iteration). A proposal may *project* outcomes with sourced math; it may not *guarantee* them.
