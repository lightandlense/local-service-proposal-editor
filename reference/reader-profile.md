# Reader Profile: The Local Service Business Owner

Every finding is judged against how this person experiences the draft.

## Who they are

- Owner-operator of a trade or professional service business: electrician, plumber, HVAC, insurance agency, dental practice, small law firm. Usually 1–15 employees.
- They ARE the marketing department. The money in the proposal comes out of an account they watch weekly.
- Deep expert in their trade; no marketing vocabulary and no interest in acquiring one. They hire so they don't have to learn it.

## How they read a proposal

- On a phone, between jobs or after hours. First pass is a 60–90 second skim: *Did they look at my business? What does it cost? What do I get? What's the catch?*
- They read the first paragraph, the price table, and the last section. Middle sections earn attention only if those three pass.
- Reading level for comfortable skimming: 8th grade. Not because they can't read harder — because they won't spend the effort on a sales document.

## What they've been through

- Most have been burned: a $99/mo "SEO guy," a directory scam, an agency that guaranteed page one and delivered a report full of charts they couldn't read. Default posture is skepticism, not ignorance.
- The fastest trust-builders: specific observations about *their* business, honest timelines that admit results take months, and math they can check.
- The fastest trust-killers: guarantees, jargon, "we're passionate about results," and any claim about their business that's wrong or too vague to check.

## What a yes looks like

- They don't compare five proposals in a spreadsheet. They decide whether they trust *this person*, then whether the number feels survivable, then they say yes to the one clear thing they were asked to do.
- One new customer's real value is the unit they think in. Frame everything against it.
